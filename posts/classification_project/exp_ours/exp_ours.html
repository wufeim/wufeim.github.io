Experiment: Our Approach

Our approach: training with new labels $c_{ij}$. Since each model easily reaches over 99.5% accuracy on the training set, almost all samples are in the diagonal of the confusion matrix.

  • Design 01. Instead of training $\Theta_t$ until convergence, we train $\Theta_t$ for only $m$ epochs, and use the prediction of $\Theta_t$ to supervise $\Theta_{t+1}$.
  • Design 02. We split the training data into 5 splits $\{S_1, \dots, S_5\}$. We start by training $\Theta_1$ on the $S_1$, and then $\Theta_2$ on $S_1, S_2$, and so on.
  • Design 03. We train a series of networks. Each network minimizes the 100-class KL divergence with the pervious network and the 10-class cross-entropy loss.

Results - Design 01

  1. We achieve very small improvements by training a series of born-again networks (BAN). However, similar performance may be achieved by training multiple times with different initializations.

Results - Design 03

Approach. We train three networks and 100 epochs each. Each network minimizes the 100-class KL divergence with the pervious network and the 10-class cross-entropy loss. This serves as a comparison with the previous two experiments as the 100-class classification are learnt in an unsupervised manner.

Results.

  1. Training a 100-class classification model without re-labeling harms the performance.
  2. Ensembling multiple teacher/student networks effectively improves the performance.
