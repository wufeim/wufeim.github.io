Experiment: Born-Again Networks

Following Furlanello et al. [1], we train a series network and each of the network $\Theta_t$ minimizes the KL divergence with the previous network $\Theta_{t-1}$ as well as the cross-entropy with the ground truth label: \[ \begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_{\Theta_1} & = \mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}, \Theta_1} \\ \mathcal{L}_{\Theta_2} & = \alpha \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}, \Theta_2} + (1-\alpha) \cdot D_\text{KL}(p_2 \mid\mid p_1) \\ \mathcal{L}_{\Theta_3} & = \alpha \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}, \Theta_3} + (1-\alpha) \cdot D_\text{KL}(p_3 \mid\mid p_2) \end{align*} \]

Results

  1. We achieve very small improvements by training a series of born-again networks (BAN). However, similar performance may be achieved by training multiple times with different initializations.
  2. Ensembling the three trained networks gives a significant performance improvement.
  3. This is consistent with the results on CIFAR-10 reported in the original BAN paper. The authors observed larger performance improvements on CIFAR-100.
