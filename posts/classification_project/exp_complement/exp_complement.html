Questions

  1. Analysis of which images are contributing to errors in Net1 and Net 2. Are Net1 and Net2 un synch or undoing each other?
  2. Run 5(b) on LeNet
  3. Increase dataset/architecture complexities
  4. O(C^^2) Classifiers, one for each major term in the confusion matrix (i.e., can we improve accuracy further by replacing one Net2 with multiple Net2's), or other ways of using intermediate number of classifiers, e.g, one per row
  5. For a give dataset and accuracy level, what is the tradeoff between Net1+Net2 size/learning time and Net1 size? Do we gain by replacing Net1 with simpler Net1+Net2?
  6. Later: Hierarchical classification with spectral clustering.
Basic Approach
  1. We start by training a classifier Net1 with the standard training strategy. For each image sample, Net1 produces a 10-dimensional vector $p_1$ where $\sum_i p_1(i) = 1$. Now we run the Net1 over the whole training set and obtain a confusion matrix $\mathbf{A}$. We set the entries on the main diagonal and normalize the entries per row. An example with 3 classes is shown below. \[ \mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.2 & 0.2 & 0.6 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow \begin{bmatrix} 0.0 & 0.2 & 0.6 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow \mathbf{W} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.0 & 0.25 & 0.75 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \end{bmatrix} \] The resulting matrix is used to re-weight the training samples so the Net2 would focus on the mistakes made by Net1.
  2. Now we train a new classifier Net2 and we hope that Net2 helps to classify images which Net1 is less certain about.
  3. Here's how we train Net2. Given a training sample $x$ with ground truth label $c$ and predicted label $k_1$ from Net1. The weight of this sample is given by \[ w = \mathbf{W}[c, k_1] \] Let the prediction vector from Net2 be $p_2$. For a batch of $j=1, \dots, n$ samples, the loss is given by \[ \mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{\sum_{j=1}^n w_j} \sum_{j=1}^n w_j \cdot \mathcal{L}_\text{CE}(p_2, c) \]
  4. Here's how we test the system. Given a new sample $x$, we first estimate the weight vector $w$ using the weight matrix $\mathbf{W}$ and the net1 prediction $k_1$. \[ w = [\mathbf{W}[0, k_1], \mathbf{W}[1, k_1], \dots, \mathbf{W}[9, k_1]] \] The final prediction vector is then given by \[ p = p_1 + w \cdot p_2 \]

Joint Training of Network 1 and Network 2

Iterative training. The straightforward idea is train Network 1 with the standard classification loss for $n$ epochs, and then train Network 2 with the complement loss for $n$ epochs. We iterate this procedure until both networks have been trained for 200 epochs. The experimental results are given below.

p epoch N1 train N1 test N2 train N2 test final train final test
0.3 50 77.20% 73.96% 73.28% 76.34% 79.45% 76.26%
0.3 25 77.39% 73.49% 76.35% 73.98% 79.46% 76.17%

The overall performance is comparable between iterative training and non-parallel training.

Dynaic training. Instead of having Network 1 trained on the standard classification loss, we may have Network 1 focus on the "important" samples only. Let $w$ be the weight to supervise the training of Network 2, we use $1-w$ to re-weight the training samples of Network 1. Now Network 2 not only helps to correct the mistakes made by Network 1, but also serves as an outlier detector so Network 1 can learn better hyperplanes for the inliers. \[ \begin{align*} \mathcal{L}_1 & = \frac{1}{\sum_{j=1}^n w_j} \sum_{j=1}^n w_j \cdot \mathcal{L}_\text{CE}(p_2, c) \\ \mathcal{L}_2 & = \frac{1}{\sum_{j=1}^n 1-w_j} \sum_{j=1}^n (1-w_j) \cdot \mathcal{L}_\text{CE}(p_2, c) \end{align*} \] where $w$ is given by the confusion matrix of Network 1 detailed above. The experimental results are given below.

p epoch N1 train N1 test N2 train N2 test final train final test
0.3 50 77.06% 73.60% 76.42% 73.80% 77.93% 74.79%

Although this approach improves the LeNet performance, it does not outperform the complement baseline.

Balance the Trade-Off

Previously, given a normalized row in the confusion matrix $w$ with ground truth label $i$, we have \[ w_j' = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if $j=i$} \\ \frac{1}{1-w_i} w_j & \text{if $j \neq i$} \end{cases} \] With such weights, Net2 becomes a specialized network trained to fix the mistakes of Net1. We investigate the trade-off between a fully-specialized model (like above) and being able to classify some examples well. We introduce a hyperparameter $p$ and now we have \[ w_j' = \begin{cases} p & \text{if $j=i$} \\ \frac{1-p}{1-w_i} w_j & \text{if $j \neq i$} \end{cases} \]
