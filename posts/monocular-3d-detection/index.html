Optimal Pose and Shape Estimation for Category-Level 3D Object Perception

In this work, Shi et al. [1] considered the 3D perception problem of reconstructing the pose and shape of the object given 3D sensor data picturing an object of a given category. They considered active shape models and proposed the first certifiably optimal solver, PACE, for pose and shape estimation. They also made the model robust to misdetections by adding an outlier rejection layer. By combining with a deep-learned keypoint detection, their model achieved SOTA performance on the ApolloScape dataset.

We consider the problem of estimating the 3D pose $(R, t)$ and shape of an object, where $R \in \text{SO}(3)$ and $t \in \mathbb{R}^3$. The shape of the object is represented as a combination of $K$ known CAD models $\mathcal{B}_k$: \[s(i) = \sum_{k=1}^K c_kb_k(i), \; \mathbf{c} \geq 0, \; \sum_{k=1}^K c_k = 1\]
